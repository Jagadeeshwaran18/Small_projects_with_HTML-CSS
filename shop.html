Wedding Garland

In Indian weddings, garlands, known as Jaimala or Varamala, are exchanged by the bride and groom as a symbol of mutual respect, love, and commitment. This tradition signifies the couple's acceptance of each other and the beginning of their marital journey. The garlands, often made of flowers, are not just decorative but also carry cultural and spiritual significance, representing prosperity, auspiciousness, and the enduring nature of the union. Every culture lends its unique customs to the wedding ceremony, and Indian weddings are renowned for their exuberant celebrations and elaborate rituals surrounding the union of the bride and groom. Here is what you could see floral-wise if you attend an Indian wedding, since flowers play a significant role in the decorating and ceremony involved. The bride and groom exchange flower garlands as part of the Hindu wedding rite (a ritual called jaimala or varmala). They are delicately weaved from blooming flowers, typically marigolds, jasmine, and roses, though the exact flowers used might change depending on the season. Similar to how rings are exchanged at a western wedding ceremony, this exchange has equivalent importance.Flowers are sunlight, nourishment, and medicine for the spirit; they make people better, happier, and more helpful. So, it comes as no surprise that flowers are an essential component of every Hindu ritual while bearing in mind these eternal words. These lovely blossoms are a beloved component of the ceremonies and the décor during weddings and funerals. Flowers are frequently used at Indian weddings.

Gods Garland

In Hindu tradition, garlands, or "malas," are floral or leaf-based decorations used to honor deities, guests, and at various ceremonies. They symbolize respect, blessings, and adornment, with specific flowers often associated with particular deities. Garlands play a significant role in religious rituals, particularly during worship (puja), where they are offered to idols and images.Garlands serve as offerings to the gods in magnificent temples, in domestic rituals, and in public ceremonies of devotion. The name of the Hindu worship ritual 'puja' that translates to the “flower act” emphasises the significance of flowers in the religious context.Garlands of various flowers and leaves are offered to deities and adorn their statues or images. In other religious, cultural and secular functions, flower garlands are used for decorative purposes and also presented to people as a sign of blessing and honour.
